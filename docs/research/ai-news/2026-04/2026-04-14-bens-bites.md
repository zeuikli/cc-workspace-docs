Headlines

[Claude Cowork](https://x.com/felixrieseberg/status/2042272870940987422) is now generally available - out of research preview after 12 weeks with millions of users. Also, [Claude for Word](https://x.com/claudeai/status/2042670341915295865) is in beta - draft, edit, and revise documents from the Word sidebar with edits showing up as tracked changes. (Team and Enterprise plans)

New Claude Code features - [/ultraplan](https://x.com/trq212/status/2042671370186973589) lets you build and edit a plan on the web, then run it in your terminal. The [Monitor tool](https://x.com/noahzweben/status/2042332268450963774) lets Claude watch for events in the background instead of constantly checking - saves a lot of tokens. Plus, an [advisor strategy](https://claude.com/blog/the-advisor-strategy) on the Claude dev platform that pairs Opus with Sonnet for better performance at similar/cheaper costs.

[OpenAI added a new $100 plan](https://community.openai.com/t/introducing-new-100-month-pro-tier/1378752). Their own wording is too complex, so I’m [stealing Theo’s](https://x.com/theo/status/2043078809843626093). Basically, $100 plan = 5x compute, $200 = 10x compute (where $20 plan = 1x). The bigger plans have 2x boost till May 31st, so effectively, $100 = 10x and $200 = 20x compute.

LlamaParse was built to tackle your most complex PDFs with the highest accuracy & the lowest cost. Now we want to put that to the test. Submit your ugliest PDF (dense contracts, financial tables, etc.), and judge the output vs your current OCR to win a Mac Mini.
